Price Guide
A tasting menu is a collection of several dishes in small portions, served in a single meal. It is designed to showcase the chef's skills and creativity, offering a journey through various flavors, culinary styles, and techniques. Diners experience a sequence of courses, often with the option of wine pairing, where each beverage complements the flavors of the dishes. The tasting menu aims to provide a comprehensive and immersive dining experience, exploring the chef's signature offerings and gastronomic vision.

The majority of the restaurants have tasting menu prices ranging between 150 to 550 AUD. Only a few outliers have prices above or below this range. There isn't a strong direct correlation between rank and price. In conclusion, while rank might influence the tasting menu's price to some extent, other factors likely play a significant role in pricing decisions.

Michelin Guide

What is a Michelin Star?
A Michelin Star is awarded to restaurants offering outstanding cooking. They take into account five universal criteria: the quality of the ingredients, the harmony of flavours, the mastery of techniques, the personality of the chef as expressed through their cuisine and, just as importantly, consistency both across the entire menu and over time.
Source: Michelin Guide website.
What is the difference between 1, 2 and 3 Michelin Stars?
One Michelin Star is awarded to restaurants using top quality ingredients, where dishes with distinct flavours are prepared to a consistently high standard.

Two Michelin Stars are awarded when the personality and talent of the chef are evident in their expertly crafted dishes; their food is refined and inspired.

Three Michelin Stars is the highest award, given for the superlative cooking of chefs at the peak of their profession; their cooking is elevated to an art form and some of their dishes are destined to become classics.

Source: Michelin Guide website.
What is the Michelin Green Star?
The Green Star is an accolade awarded annually. It highlights to restaurant-goers those places that distinguish themselves on account of their bold, sincere and highly committed approach to sustainable gastronomy.

Proposing a dining experience that has eco-responsibility at its core, these places are true role models, designing an alternative vision of gastronomy that is not only delicious but also virtuous.

Today, out of more than 15,000 restaurants recommended by the Michelin Guide, just over 350 have obtained a Michelin Green Star.

Source: Michelin Guide website.
